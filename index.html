Solar System

Introduction

The Solar System is the gravitationally bound system of the Sun and the objects that orbit it, either directly or indirectly. Of the objects that orbit the Sun directly, the largest are the eight planets, with the remainder being smaller objects, such as the five dwarf planets and small Solar System bodies.

Why We Need to Learn About Space

Understanding space is crucial for advancing our knowledge and technology. Physics, as the foundation of space exploration, helps us grasp fundamental principles that govern the universe. This knowledge not only drives scientific and technological innovation but also fosters global collaboration and problem-solving. By studying space, we can develop solutions to terrestrial challenges, such as resource management and environmental sustainability. Moreover, space exploration inspires future generations and promotes a sense of unity among people worldwide, reinforcing our shared responsibility to preserve and enhance life on Earth.
